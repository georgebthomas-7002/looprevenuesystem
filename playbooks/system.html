System Playbooks

Turn The Loop Revenue System Into Concrete Next Steps

The Loop Revenue System gives you a clear way to think about revenue: four loops and four stages. That is the model.

System playbooks are how you make that model real.

On this page, you will see simple, practical plays for each stage across marketing, sales, service, and ops. You can start with the stage that feels weakest or with the loop that matters most right now and still make progress.

You do not need to do everything at once. You need to choose one play, run it, learn, and then keep the loop spinning.

Tailor - Make It Personal
Amplify - Scale What Works
Evolve - Learn On Purpose

What A System Playbook Is And Why It Matters

A system playbook is a small set of repeatable actions that move your loops forward by stage. It turns questions like "How do we improve Express?" into concrete moves your team can actually make. It gives you a way to answer, "What does this look like in marketing, sales, service, and ops?" without guessing.

Good playbooks:

  • Start from the model, not from a random list of tactics.
  • Stay small enough to run in weeks, not years.
  • Connect clearly to a stage and a loop.
  • Have an owner, a metric, and a debrief.

Without playbooks, the Loop Revenue System stays theoretical. With them, it becomes a way your team works.

How To Use This Page

There are two easy ways to use this page.

Start By Stage

Pick Express, Tailor, Amplify, or Evolve and see example plays for marketing, sales, service, and ops. This is a good path if you know, for example, that your Tailor stage is weak across the board.

Start By Loop

Read each stage play for your world, such as Loop Marketing or Loop Sales, and choose one or two plays to run first. This is a good path if you lead one department and want to improve it without waiting for a full company rollout.

Either way, move slowly enough that your team can keep up, and fast enough that you actually feel momentum.

Express Playbook - Get Clear Before You Scale

Express is about clarity. Before you push harder on personalization, automation, or optimization, you need to know what you stand for, who you are serving, and how your process works. You can treat Express as a "clarity sprint" for each loop.

Express Play - Loop Marketing

Choose one core audience you care about. Spend a week listening carefully.

  • Read ten recent win notes and ten loss notes for that audience.
  • Review search queries, chat logs, and common ticket topics related to their problems.
  • Write a simple one page Express brief that answers: Who are they? What problem do they think they have? What problem do you believe they actually have? What role should your product or service play in their world?

Use that brief to update one key asset: your main page for that audience, a core landing page, or a foundational blog article.

Express Play - Loop Sales

Sit down with a few of your most experienced reps and a few newer reps.

  • Define, in plain language, what a good fit opportunity looks like.
  • Agree on entry and exit criteria for each stage of your sales process.
  • List five questions every rep should ask in discovery to protect both the buyer and your team.

Write this into a one page Express guide for sales. Use it in your next pipeline review and coaching sessions.

Express Play - Loop Service

Take your main product or offer and ask one question.

What does "success" look like for a customer 90 days after they buy?

Document this for two or three key segments. Then map a simple, high level journey from "closed won" to that 90 day success moment.

Use that journey to update your onboarding emails, kickoff calls, or in app guidance.

Express Play - Loop Ops

Bring one leader from marketing, sales, and service together.

  • On a whiteboard or in a doc, sketch your ideal lifecycle from "first seen" to "renewal or expansion."
  • Name the stages. Write one sentence for what each stage means.
  • List the minimum fields you need to track that lifecycle reliably.

Push this into your CRM or core tool. Use it as the backbone for how you name and manage lifecycle fields.

Tailor Playbook - Make It Personal And Contextual

Tailor is about relevance. Once you are clear on Express, you can adjust your messages, flows, and systems to match what real people need. This is where a lot of simple personalization goes wrong. The goal is not to use every data point. The goal is to be more helpful.

Tailor Play - Loop Marketing

Pick one high value segment, such as customers in a specific industry or role.

Audit one of your main journeys for that segment, such as a nurture sequence or a content path on your site. Ask three questions:

  • Does the language match how they actually talk about their problem?
  • Are we answering the next question they usually have, or just what we want to say?
  • Do we send the same thing to everyone, or does some behavior change what they see next?

Make two improvements. Adjust language in one key asset and change one rule in your nurture or content logic to better fit this segment.

Tailor Play - Loop Sales

Choose one target segment where your win rate should be higher.

Before each meeting, review the account's firmographics, digital behavior, and any support or product usage for existing contacts.

Update your discovery and proposal template for that segment with:

  • Two segment specific questions.
  • One segment specific risk to discuss openly.
  • One segment specific example or customer story.

Ask reps to use these in their next set of calls and record what changes in the conversation.

Tailor Play - Loop Service

Identify a group of customers who often struggle early, such as smaller teams or customers in a specific vertical.

Look at their first thirty days:

  • What tickets do they open?
  • Where do they drop off in onboarding?
  • What do they tell your team in calls or surveys?

Create a simple, tailored onboarding add-on for this group, such as an extra check in call, a short video, or a targeted checklist. Track whether this changes their early experience.

Tailor Play - Loop Ops

Pick one process where people complain about "too many clicks" or "having to repeat information."

Follow a handful of records through that process. Ask:

  • Do we have the fields we need to route and report on this work?
  • Are we asking for the same data twice?
  • Could we pre fill or infer some fields based on existing information?

Make one configuration change that reduces friction without losing needed data.

Amplify Playbook - Scale What Works Without Burning Out

Amplify is where you take what is working in one corner and make it easier for more people to use. You do not start Amplify with random tactics. You start it with something that has already shown promise in Express and Tailor.

Amplify Play - Loop Marketing

Find one asset that consistently performs well. It might be a blog post, a webinar, a playbook, or a video.

Turn it into:

  • A short email sequence for new contacts who care about that topic.
  • A set of social posts that highlight the main ideas.
  • A short guide or checklist your sales team can send.

Do not change the core idea. Change the format and channels.

Amplify Play - Loop Sales

Identify a rep, message, or motion that outperforms the team average.

Capture the specifics. Record a call (with consent), copy the exact email language, and document the sequence of steps.

Build:

  • A simple playbook in your CRM or sales tool.
  • One core sequence that reflects this motion.
  • A short internal training session where the rep walks through the why, not just the what.

Encourage reps to try this play in the right scenarios, not everywhere.

Amplify Play - Loop Service

Find a question your team answers at least five times a week.

  • Write a clear, plain language article that answers it with screenshots or a short video.
  • Add it to your knowledge base and tag it properly.
  • Update your internal macros or saved replies to link to this resource.

Over time, track how often this resource is used and whether related ticket volume drops.

Amplify Play - Loop Ops

Identify one manual process that is boring, predictable, and rule based.

  • Map the steps. Check that the rules are clear and stable.
  • Automate one or two of those steps in your main system, such as assignment, notifications, or field updates.
  • Communicate clearly what changed, why, and how to get help if something looks wrong.

Use this as a template to automate similar patterns elsewhere.

Evolve Playbook - Learn On Purpose, Not By Accident

Evolve turns motion into improvement. This is where you look back at the plays you ran in Express, Tailor, and Amplify and decide what to keep, what to change, and what to drop.

Evolve Play - Loop Marketing

Choose one campaign, content series, or nurture sequence you have been running.

Ask:

  • Which stage and loop was this meant to improve?
  • What metrics did we expect to move?
  • What actually changed?

Bring one person from sales and one from service into the review. Ask what they saw on their side. Then decide: Do we keep this as is? Do we adjust and run another cycle? Do we retire it and try something else?

Capture a short narrative of what you learned.

Evolve Play - Loop Sales

Look at the last month or quarter of deals for a specific segment.

  • Group them into wins, losses, and no decisions.
  • Write down the most common reasons in each bucket.
  • Compare this to what your team writes in the CRM today.

Update your reason picklist or tags so they match reality. Use this new view in your next leadership meeting and in your next sales training.

Evolve Play - Loop Service

Take a period of time, like the last 60 or 90 days.

Look at:

  • Top ticket categories.
  • Time to first response and time to resolution.
  • Retention and expansion for cohorts that had heavy support use.

Identify one pattern that clearly harms customer outcomes. Work with product, marketing, or sales to propose a small change. Maybe you update a feature, a help article, a promise, or an onboarding step.

Review the impact after another cycle.

Evolve Play - Loop Ops

Review your system change log for the last quarter.

  • List the changes that had a clear positive impact on loop metrics, such as better data, faster cycle times, or fewer errors.
  • List the changes that created confusion or did not land.

Ask: What do our best changes have in common? What do our worst changes have in common?

Use that insight to refine your change process, such as stronger requirements, more testing, or better communication.

Experiments And Iteration Inside Playbooks

Every play on this page can be run as a small experiment. You do not have to commit forever up front.

You can:

  • Write a simple hypothesis.
  • Name the loop and stage.
  • Pick one or two metrics that should move.
  • Timebox the experiment, such as four or eight weeks.
Hypothesis Stage Loop Metric Timebox

At the end of the timebox, review what happened, decide what to keep, and move on. This way, the Loop Revenue System does not turn into a big, fixed program. It becomes a rhythm of smaller experiments, grounded in the same model, that help your whole business adjust and flourish over time.

Where AI Fits In These Playbooks And Where Humans Lead

AI can make most of these plays faster and more comfortable to run.

AI Can Accelerate

  • Summarize calls, scan feedback, and draft Express briefs.
  • Generate segment specific language and test variations in Tailor.
  • Repurpose content and orchestrate automation in Amplify.
  • Spot patterns in data during Evolve.

Humans Must Decide

  • Choose which problems to focus on.
  • Set boundaries for personalization and automation.
  • Decide which plays to keep and which to stop.
  • Own the impact of changes on customers and teams.
